**Access Control Policy**

**1\. Purpose  
This Access Control Policy defines the principles, requirements, and responsibilities for managing access to organizational information systems to protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability, in alignment with NIST 800-53 guidelines.**

**2\. Scope  
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and other third parties who access the organizationâ€™s information systems, including hardware, software, cloud services, and physical resources.**

**3\. Policy Statement**

**3.1 Access Control Principles**

- **Access to information systems shall be granted based on the principles of least privilege and need-to-know.**
- **Role-based access controls (RBAC) shall be implemented to ensure users only have permissions aligned with their job responsibilities.**

**3.2 Account Management**

- **All user accounts must be uniquely identifiable.**
- **The creation, modification, and deletion of accounts must follow documented approval workflows.**
- **Privileged accounts, such as administrator accounts, must be restricted to authorized personnel and monitored regularly.**

**3.3 Authentication and Authorization**

- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA) shall be required for all access to critical systems, remote access, and privileged accounts.**
- **Passwords must meet organizational complexity and rotation requirements, including a minimum length of 12 characters and regular changes every 90 days.**
- **Authentication mechanisms must comply with NIST SP 800-63 guidelines.**

**3.4 Remote Access**

- **Remote access to systems shall be permitted only through approved, secure channels such as Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) or NIST recognized encryption standard through remote access software.**
- **All remote access activities shall be logged and monitored for anomalies.**

**3.5 Access Reviews and Audits**

- **User access rights must be reviewed at least quarterly to verify alignment with job roles.**
- **Inactive accounts must be disabled after 30 days of inactivity and removed after 90 days unless otherwise approved.**

**3.6 Session Management**

- **Systems shall enforce automatic session timeouts after 15 minutes of inactivity.**
- **Users must log off or lock their workstations when leaving them unattended.**

**3.7 Third-Party Access**

- **Third parties requiring access to organizational systems must sign non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) and follow all relevant security policies.**
- **Access for third parties must be restricted, temporary, and monitored.**

**4\. Roles and Responsibilities**

**4.1 System Owners**

- **Approve and manage access to their respective systems.**

**4.2 System Administrators**

- **Implement access control measures and maintain logs of access activities.**

**4.3 Security Team**

- **Conduct regular access audits and monitor compliance with this policy.**

**4.4 All Users**

- **Comply with this policy and report any unauthorized access attempts immediately.**

**5\. Enforcement  
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary actions, up to and including termination, and/or legal action.**

**6\. Review and Revision  
This policy shall be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in organizational structure, systems, or regulatory requirements.**
